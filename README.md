# Survive-The-Semester
PROBLEM STATEMENT:
In many Computer Science learning environments, students engage with practice questions and assignments as part of their learning process, but the experience remains largely unstructured and lacks meaningful feedback. While students receive scores or completion status, they are not provided with clear insights into their conceptual understanding at a topic or chapter level.
This makes it difficult for learners to identify their strengths and areas that require improvement, often leading to repeated mistakes and superficial learning. Additionally, there is a lack of systems that continuously track learning progress, analyze patterns in student performance, and provide actionable guidance for improvement.
As a result, students are unable to learn at their own pace or focus effectively on weak areas, leading to inefficient learning and incomplete conceptual clarity.
